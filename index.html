üõ†Ô∏è The Setup

1
Grab OpCore-Simplify and let it build your EFI folder (it's like magic! ‚ú®).
2
Fire up USB Toolbox to map your ports (don't skip this!).
3
Drop the generated kexts into EFI > OC > Kext. Easy peasy.
4
Open OCAT and hit Ctrl+O to load your config.plist.
5
Go to Kext, click "+", and add those new files.
6
Now for the big boss battle: USB Mapping! ‚ö†Ô∏è

‚ö†Ô∏è USB Magic (Important!)

üö´ SERIOUSLY, DON'T SKIP THIS

Bad USB mapping = Broken Sleep + Random Crashes. Do it right, do it once. üõ°Ô∏è

Why bother?

  • 15 Port Limit: macOS hates having too many USB ports.
  • Sleep/Wake: Fixes insomnia for your PC.
  • Stability: Keeps kernel panics away.
  • Bluetooth/WiFi: Needs proper mapping to fly.
  • iServices: FaceTime needs this to see your beautiful face.

USBToolBox Method (The Easy Way)

üîå USBToolBox Cheat Sheet
# Windows: Run Windows.exe # macOS: python3 USBToolBox.py # The Game Plan: # 1. Press "D" to find ports # 2. Plug stuff into EVERY port # 3. Press "S" to pick the winners (max 15) # 4. Press "K" to get your kext # 5. Profit (Copy UTBMap.kext to EFI)
1
Open USBToolBox and smash "D" to discover ports.
2
Plug a device into EVERY port (yes, even the dusty ones).
3
Hit "S" and pick your ports (keep it under 15, macOS rules ü§∑‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è).
4
Press "K" to bake your UTBMap.kext.
5
Copy that fresh kext to EFI/OC/Kexts/ and update your config.
üö´ One Last Thing...

After mapping, go to Kernel > Quirks in your config and kill XhciPortLimit (Set it to False). Trust me.
